How to Use the System

Simple step-by-step instructions for families, Intore mu Ikoranabuhanga and authorities.

1. Accessing the Platform

This is mimic instructional text. Families can access the platform by visiting www.duhigure.rw on a smartphone, tablet or computer. If a family does not have access to the internet, they can be supported by an Intore mu Ikoranabuhanga or by the cell/sector administration.

2. Creating or Identifying a Family Record

  1. Open the Family Portal from the top menu.
  2. Search by family code or names to check if the family already exists.
  3. If not found, click on Register New Family (see the form on the portal page).

3. Registering Family Members

In the registration form, capture all required information for each member, such as full name, date of birth, gender, national ID (if available), relation to head of family, education level and occupation. Use the “Add Member” button to add multiple members in the same family.

4. Setting Performance Duties (Imihigo)

Each family member can have individual duties. The whole family can also have shared duties that every member contributes to. In the portal, use the “Individual Duties” section for member-specific goals and the “Shared Family Duties” section for goals that are common to everyone.

  • Example individual duty: “Child attends school every day and completes homework.”
  • Example family duty: “Construct and use an improved latrine by June.”

5. Updating Progress

Periodically, the family or supporting staff can log in to update the status of each duty (Not started, In progress, Completed). Comments can be added to explain challenges or achievements.

6. Roles and Responsibilities

Families

Provide accurate information, participate in planning, and follow up on agreed duties.

Intore mu Ikoranabuhanga

Support families in using the system, ensure completeness and correctness of data, and report any technical issues.

Administrative Authorities

Monitor progress at cell/sector level, provide feedback and guidance, and use reports from the system to inform local planning.

Quick Tips

  • Prepare all family information before starting registration.
  • Use clear and measurable wording for performance duties.
  • Update progress regularly instead of waiting until the end of the year.
  • Contact your Intore mu Ikoranabuhanga if you get stuck.
